Chiles en Nogada

Chiles en nogada is one of Mexico’s most emblematic dishes, known for its balance of savory, sweet, and creamy flavors, as well as its striking tricolor presentation that mirrors the Mexican flag. The dish starts with roasted poblano chiles that are carefully peeled to keep their structure intact. These chiles are then filled with picadillo, a warm, aromatic mixture of ground or finely chopped meat combined with seasonal fruits like apple, pear, and peach, plus nuts, spices, and sometimes candied cactus.

The defining element is the nogada, a silky walnut cream sauce made from fresh walnuts, milk or cream, a touch of sherry, and subtle spices. When properly prepared, the sauce is thick, slightly sweet, and naturally pale — not drowned in sugar and not overly blended; the goal is a delicate, almost velvety texture.

The dish is finished with pomegranate seeds and fresh parsley, delivering a burst of acidity and herbaceous freshness on top of the rich cream and savory filling. Traditionally served at room temperature, chiles en nogada is a seasonal dish associated with late summer and early fall, when pomegranates and fresh walnuts are at their peak.

Ingredients

For the chiles

  • 6 poblano peppers
  • Oil (for lightly roasting)

For the picadillo (filling)

  • 500g/1kg ground beef (Depending poblano pepper size)
  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 2 garlic cloves, minced
  • 2 Roma tomatoes, chopped
  • 1 apple, peeled and diced
  • 1 pear, peeled and diced
  • 1 peach, peeled and diced
  • ¼ cup raisins
  • ¼ cup silvered almonds
  • Salt
  • Pepper
  • Optional spices:cinnamon, clove, bay leaf

For the nogada (walnut sauce)

  • 1 cup walnuts (peeled and soaked; avoid bitterness)
  • 1 cup milk or cream
  • 100g fresh cheese
  • 1 tablespoon shugar
  • Pinch if salt

For garnish

  • Pomegranate seeds
  • Fresh parsley, chopped

Steps

Roast and prepare the chiles

  1. Roast the poblano peppers over fire or in a pan until the skin blisters.
  2. Place them in a plastic bag for 10 minutes to steam.
  3. Peel the skin carefully.
  4. Make a small cut on the side and remove seeds.
  5. Set aside.

Make the picadillo

  1. In a pan, cook the ground beef until browned.
  2. Add onion + garlic; cook until soft.
  3. Add tomatoes and cook until the mixture thickens.
  4. Add diced apple, pear, and peach.
  5. Add raisins, almonds, salt, pepper, and a small touch of cinnamon or clove if you want.
  6. Cook until the fruit softens slightly — not mushy.
  7. Taste and adjust seasoning.

Make the nogada

  1. Blend the walnuts with milk (or cream)
  2. Add fresh cheese
  3. Add sugar + a bit of salt.
  4. Blend it until smooth
  5. Adjust thickness:
    • Too thick → add more milk
    • Too thin → add more walnut or cheese

Serve

  1. Stuff each chile with picadillo.
  2. Place them on a plate.
  3. Pour the nogada sauce over the top.
  4. Sprinkle pomegranate seeds.
  5. Add chopped parsley.
