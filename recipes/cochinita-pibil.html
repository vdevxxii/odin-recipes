Cochinita Pibil

Cochinita pibil is slow-cooked pork from Yucatán, marinated in achiote (a deep-red spice paste), sour orange juice, garlic, and spices, then wrapped in banana leaves and cooked until it falls apart. The flavor is earthy, citrusy, slightly smoky, and intensely aromatic.

Served shredded with pickled red onions and usually eaten in tacos, tortas, or by the plate. It’s one of the most iconic dishes of southern Mexico — simple ingredients, deep flavor chemistry.

Ingredients

  • Pork shoulder
  • Banana leaves
  • Achiote paste
  • Sour orange juice
  • Garlic
  • White vinegar
  • Cumin
  • Oregano
  • Black pepper
  • Salt
  • Cloves (optional)
  • Cinnamon sticks
  • Habanero
  • Red Onions
  • Tortillas
